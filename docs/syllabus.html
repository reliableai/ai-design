Syllabus & Course Info

Course Description

This course explores investigative techniques through the lens of Sherlock Holmes stories. We cover observational skills, logical reasoning, forensic science basics, and critical thinking. Students will solve fictional cases inspired by the adventures of Sherlock Holmes while learning real-world analytical skills.

Learning Objectives

  • Develop keen observation and attention to detail.
  • Apply deductive reasoning to solve complex problems.
  • Understand basic principles of forensic science and evidence analysis.
  • Work collaboratively to crack challenging "cases" (assignments).

Course Materials

Main Text: The Complete Sherlock Holmes by Sir Arthur Conan Doyle (selected stories). Supplementary readings will be provided as links or handouts (see Resources page).

Grading

  • Assignments (case reports) – 50%
  • Midterm Mystery Challenge – 20%
  • Final Case Presentation – 25%
  • Participation (class discussion, forum) – 5%

Policies

Late Work: Cases (assignments) must be submitted by the deadline. Extensions are granted only for true emergencies (with evidence as solid as Holmes’ deductions!).

Honor Code: Collaboration is encouraged for brainstorming, but your write-ups must be your own. Any outside sources or sleuthing help must be cited. Plagiarism or cheating will be treated as a serious infraction (no sleight of hand allowed).
